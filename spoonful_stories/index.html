Gulabjamun

About Meal

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert consisting of deep-fried dough balls soaked in a sweet syrup, known for its rich, sweet, and indulgent flavor.

Ingredients:


Milk Powder
All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
Ghee (Clarified Butter)
Milk
Baking Soda
Oil (for deep frying)
Sugar
Water
Cardamom Powder
Rose Water (optional)
Chopped Nuts (for garnish, optional)
Saffron Strands (for garnish, optional)

Instructions:


Prepare dough with milk powder.
Form small smooth balls.
Heat oil for deep frying.
Fry balls until golden brown.
Make sugar syrup: sugar, water.
Add cardamom, rose water optionally.
Soak fried balls in syrup.
Let them absorb syrup.
Serve warm or at room temperature.
Garnish with nuts, saffron strands.

Mutton

About Meal

Mutton curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish made from tender pieces of mutton (goat or lamb) cooked in a rich and spiced gravy, often enjoyed in various regional styles around the world.

Ingredients:



Mutton (goat or lamb)
Onions
Tomatoes
Garlic
Ginger
Cooking Oil or Ghee
Spices (coriander, cumin, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala)
Yogurt or Cream
Water or Broth
Salt
Fresh Herbs (e.g., cilantro)
Optional Ingredients (e.g., green chilies, cardamom, bay leaves)

Instruction:


1. Rub salt, ground black pepper, and garlic powder on the pork tenderloin chop. Let it stay for at least 30 minutes.
2. Make the gravy by melting 1 tablespoon butter in a saucepan. When the butter melts, gradually add the flour and whisk until the color turns light brown. Pour-in the beef broth and stir. Add salt and pepper according to taste. Continue cooking until the texture becomes thick. Set aside.
3. Heat a skillet of frying pan then put-in 2 tablespoons of butter and let melt.
4. Pan-fry the pork loin chop in medium heat until the color of each side turns light brown (approximately 7 to 8 minutes per side). Set aside.
5. Heat a sizzling plate or fajita plate then put-in 1 tablespoon butter.
6. Distribute the butter around the plate then arrange rice, mixed vegetables, and pork loin chop.
7. Pour gravy over the pork loin chop then turn-off heat.
8. Serve. Share and enjoy!

Sambar

About Meal

Sambar is a South Indian lentil-based stew enriched with vegetables and aromatic spices, known for its tangy and savory flavor.

Ingredients:


Toor Dal (Split Pigeon Peas)
Tamarind Pulp
Mixed Vegetables (e.g., drumsticks, okra, carrots, eggplant)
Sambar Powder (spice mix)
Turmeric Powder
Asafoetida (Hing)
Mustard Seeds
Curry Leaves
Red Chili Powder (optional)
Coriander Leaves (Cilantro)
Cooking Oil
Salt

Instructions:


Cook toor dal (pigeon peas) until soft.
Soak tamarind in warm water, extract pulp.
Boil mixed vegetables until tender.
Heat oil, add mustard seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves.
Sauté vegetables, add sambar powder, turmeric.
Add tamarind pulp, salt, and cooked dal.
Simmer until flavors meld. Garnish with cilantro. Serve.

Fries

About Meal

French fries are crispy and golden potato strips, deep-fried to perfection, and seasoned with salt, enjoyed for their classic, savory taste and crispy texture.

Ingredients:


Potatoes
Cooking Oil (e.g., vegetable oil)
Salt

Instructions:


Wash, peel, and cut potatoes into strips.
Soak in cold water to remove starch.
Drain and pat dry with paper towels.
Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot.
Fry potatoes in batches until golden.
Remove, drain excess oil on paper.
Sprinkle with salt. Serve hot. Enjoy!
